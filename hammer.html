Product Requirements Document (PRD)

V2RBuilds – On-Site AI Estimate & Negotiation Assistant

Document Version: 1.0

Owner: Product (V2RBuilds)

Last Updated: 2025-12-10

1. Product Overview

V2RBuilds aims to give general contractors—especially Brazilian, Spanish-speaking, and other non-native English speakers—a competitive on-site closing tool that produces accurate, itemized renovation estimates in real time.

The tool reduces friction in negotiation, enhances contractor credibility, and bridges communication gaps with homeowners.

This PRD defines the workflow, functionality, UI/UX expectations, and liability considerations for the AI-powered Estimate & Negotiation Assistant.

2. Problem Statement

General contractors (GCs) routinely lose deals because:

  • They cannot generate polished estimates immediately on-site.
  • Language barriers limit negotiation effectiveness.
  • Homeowners frequently undervalue the actual cost of work.
  • Leaving the job site to “calculate and return later” reduces close rate and undermines trust.

Contractors need a fast, professional, guided tool that:

  1. Records job details on-site,
  2. Converts them into an itemized estimate instantly,
  3. Helps navigate budget misalignment and negotiation,
  4. Presents a credible, polished output to the homeowner.

3. Goals & Success Criteria

3.1 Product Goals

  • Enable contractors to produce an accurate, professional, itemized estimate within minutes of finishing the initial walkthrough.
  • Improve contractor win rates during first visit (“one-call close”).
  • Reduce negotiation disadvantage driven by language barriers.
  • Gather structured job data for future automations and pipeline insights.

3.2 Success Metrics

  • Time to estimate < 5 minutes from end of recording.
  • Contractor approval rate > 90% (usefulness signal).
  • Homeowner acceptance/close rate increases by 20–40%.
  • Daily active usage among contractors generating ≥2 estimates/day.

4. User Personas

Primary User: General Contractor (GC)

  • Often bilingual but not fluent in English.
  • Needs fast turnaround, negotiation support, and credibility boosts.
  • Familiar with rough cost structures already.

Secondary User: Homeowner

  • Age 40–65 (low tech adoption).
  • High sensitivity to estimate presentation and perceived contractor professionalism.
  • Typically unaware of real pricing for renovations.

5. Product Scope

5.1 Workflow Requirements

Final Chosen Approach: Pedro’s “Hammer Approach” — real-time on-site estimate generation.

The estimate is generated after the homeowner conversation but before leaving the house, enabling immediate negotiation.

Workflow Steps

  1. Contractor interviews homeowner (normal conversation).
  2. Openly record a structured voice memo in contractor’s preferred language.
  3. AI analyzes recording in real time, extracting:
    • Full scope of work
    • Materials & quality level
    • Square footage / linear footage
    • Labor needs
    • Timeline expectations
  4. AI generates itemized estimate, including:
    • Materials
    • Labor
    • Hours/timeline
    • GC margin (40–60% editable)
    • Total cost range & recommended price
  5. Contractor must review + approve before sharing.
  6. Contractor sends or screenshares polished estimate to homeowner for negotiation.

6. Core Functional Requirements

6.1 Voice Recording

  • Recordings must support:
    • Portuguese (Brazilian)
    • Spanish
    • English
  • Transcription and translation required.
  • Ability to tag different rooms or tasks verbally.

Requirements:

  • ≤ 60 minutes of recording.
  • Immediate upload and processing.
  • Offline fallback mode queued for upload later.

6.2 Real-Time Estimate Generation

Outputs Required

  • Itemized line-by-line breakdown
  • Material grade sliders (good / better / best)
  • Labor hours + timeline prediction
  • Profit margin visibility (40–60% default, editable)
  • Total cost + recommended quote
  • Optional: range-based pricing to protect negotiation flexibility

Technical Spec

  • Must generate estimate in < 60 seconds for a typical job.
  • Rates database configurable per region.
  • Contractor-specific historical pricing learning model (future version).

6.3 Contractor Validation Gate

Before sending the estimate to homeowner, UI must include:

  • “Approve & Share” button
  • “Edit Line Items” modal
  • “Recalculate with Adjusted Scope” option

Contractor acknowledgement:

“I accept responsibility for this estimate. V2RBuilds is not liable for pricing inaccuracies.”

They must tap Agree to proceed.

7. Negotiation Facilitation Features

7.1 Strategic Question Prompts (“Sales Coach Mode”)

Before estimate generation, the app prompts GC to ask:

  • “What budget range are you hoping to stay within?”
  • “Have you gotten any other quotes yet?”
  • “When were you hoping to start?”
  • “What materials or brands do you prefer?”

Purpose: Extract homeowner constraints before showing pricing.

7.2 Professional Presentation Layer

Estimate delivered in a clean, modern, PDF-like format:

  • Logo & branding
  • Room-by-room structure
  • Clear totals
  • Options A/B/C for scope/quality

Goal: elevate contractor credibility instantly.

7.3 Scope Adjustment Sliders

UI allows contractor to vary:

  • Tile quality
  • Flooring type
  • Demo or no demo
  • Baseboard removal
  • Paint grade
  • Plumbing/electrical complexity

Sliders recompute estimate in real time.

7.4 Sales Scripts for Non-Native Speakers

Built-in scripts for:

  • Explaining cost differences
  • Anchoring homeowner expectations
  • Handling objections
  • Closing techniques for on-site deals

Must be simple, one-tap prompts.

8. Market Dynamics Considerations

8.1 Negotiation Patterns

Insights the app must accommodate:

  • Certain homeowner ethnic groups (e.g., Chinese) negotiate heavily.
  • Some homeowners negotiate despite lacking actual budget.
  • Contractors must rapidly identify whether the job is winnable.

Feature Implications

  • Add “Budget Alignment Score” (low/medium/high) based on conversation cues.
  • Color-coded warning if expectations appear unrealistic.

8.2 Reality Gap Management

Homeowners often underestimate costs by 40–80%.

App must:

  • Flag unrealistic expectations.
  • Help GC explain variance through:
    • Visual breakdown
    • Labor/material education modules

9. Legal & Liability Requirements

9.1 Contractor Responsibility Disclaimer

Every estimate requires the contractor to acknowledge:

  • They reviewed the output.
  • They adjusted pricing where necessary.
  • V2RBuilds is not liable for errors, omissions, or underbids.

9.2 Data Handling

  • All recordings stored securely and encrypted.
  • Contractor must opt-in to recording.
  • For states requiring dual consent, app must surface legal notice.

10. Non-Functional Requirements

  • Speed: Estimate generation < 60 seconds.
  • Reliability: 99% uptime for recording + transcription service.
  • Accuracy: Must hit ±15% accuracy from experienced GC’s assessed quote.
  • Usability: Designed for contractors with limited tech familiarity.
  • Accessibility: Large buttons, bilingual UI, simple flow.

11. Out of Scope (for V1)

  • Multi-trade collaboration estimates.
  • Homeowner self-service mode.
  • Permitting workflows.
  • Materials ordering/inventory integration.
  • Payment collection or contract signing.

12. Future Enhancements

  • “Find Me Leads” — homeowner/contractor matching (“dating app”)
  • Historical cost training per GC
  • Integrated CRM and job pipeline
  • AI-driven photo recognition for scope validation
  • Multi-version quotes (Good/Better/Best) auto-generation
  • Dynamic negotiation assistant responsive to homeowner reactions
